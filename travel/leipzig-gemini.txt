Once you step out of Leipzig Hbf, you are only a 10-minute walk from the historic center. 
Follow the Leipzig Music Trail (look for the curved steel inserts in the pavement).

1. St. Thomas Church (Thomaskirche) — The "Must-Visit"
Why: This is where Johann Sebastian Bach worked as a cantor for 27 years.
The Grave: Bach’s bronze tomb is located prominently in the sanctuary floor near the altar.
Bonus: There is a large Bach statue outside, and the Bach Museum is directly across from the church entrance.

2. Mendelssohn House
Why: The final private residence of Felix Mendelssohn Bartholdy, preserved as it was in the 1840s.
What to see: His study, the salon where he hosted "musical Sundays," and an interactive "Effektorium" where you can "conduct" a virtual orchestra.

3. Schumann House
Why: This is where Robert and Clara Schumann spent the first four years of their marriage.
What to see: A beautiful museum dedicated to their partnership and Clara’s career as a world-class pianist.

4. St. Nicholas Church (Nikolaikirche)
Why: While famous for the "Peaceful Revolution" of 1989, it was also one of Bach’s main performance venues. It has a stunning, palm-tree-style interior.

5. The Gewandhaus & Opera House
Location: Both are located on Augustusplatz.
The Vibe: The Gewandhaus is home to one of the world's oldest and most prestigious orchestras. Even if you don't see a show, the architecture and the massive mural in the lobby are worth a look.

The "Deep Dive" Grave: Alter Johannisfriedhof
--
If you are specifically looking for musician graves, walk (or take a short tram) to the Alter Johannisfriedhof (Old St. John’s Cemetery).

Musician Interest: This was Bach's original burial place before he was moved to St. Thomas. 9
You can also find the graves of other notable Leipzig figures here. It is a very peaceful, park-like setting.

Public Transport: Most sites are within a 20-minute walk of each other. 
If you're tired, the Tram 4, 7, or 15 will zip you between Augustusplatz and the Central Station.

Timing: Try to visit on a Friday (6:00 PM) or Saturday (3:00 PM) if you want to hear the world-famous St. 
Thomas Boys Choir (Thomanerchor) perform in the church for a small fee.

Food: Have lunch at Auerbachs Keller, a historic restaurant famous for being the setting of a scene in Goethe's Faust.
